Families:

__FORCETOC__ Within the OOMP ID system there are also families of components. These are common components available in either a variety of colours or values.
Each family shares four common elements, either:
TYPE - SIZE - COLOR - INDEX

(ex.
CRHO-I01-B-###-01 - 2.54mm XX Pin Black Crimp Housing
RESE-W04-X-###-01 - 1/4 Watt XXXX Ohm Resistor

Or:
TYPE - SIZE - DESCRIPTION - INDEX

(ex.
LEDS-10-#-FROS-01 - 10 mm Frosted XXX LED
WIRS-G28-#-STAN-01 - 28 AWG XXX Stranded Wire


Each family of components is assigned either a two or three charachter hexadecimal name space, then the following one to three charachters are used to describe the individual components value.
(ex.
CRHO-I01-B
Namespace: DA##
CRHO-I01-B-04PI-01 2.54mm 4 Pin Black Crimp Housing HEX: DA04
CRHO-I01-B-08PI-01 2.54mm 8 Pin Black Crimp Housing HEX: DA08
CRHO-I01-B-10PI-01 2.54mm 10 Pin Black Crimp Housing HEX: DA0A
RESE-W04-X
Namespace: AE###
RESE-W04-X-O561-01 1/4 Watt 560 Ohm Resistor HEX: AE561
RESE-W04-X-O103-01 1/4 Watt 10 k Ohm Resistor HEX: AE103

These little plastic bits are everywhere. They're Used for connecting wires to 2.54 mm (HEAD-I01, HEAL-I01) headers that are found on almost every PCB. While very handy, they are not keyed, so it's easy to plug them in backwards. Because of this it's best to avoid them when frequent connecting/un-connecting is required, or the design is to be used by untrained persons. While these connectors are everywhere they are (slowly) falling out of favor, being replaced by finer pitch connectors that allow denser connections.
